#01 — The Problem

Listing records
sucks.

You know the drill. Pull the sleeve. Type the title. Copy the catalog number off the spine. Cross-reference Discogs. Write a description. Re-write it for Shopify. Re-write it for the marketplace. Photograph it five times. Screw up the matrix number. Repeat. Four hundred times.

01 / Manual entry

You're a human OCR machine.

Typing titles, artists, labels, catalog numbers and pressing details by hand. For every record. Errors compound. Listings get rejected.

02 / Cross-platform chaos

Different rules on every channel.

Discogs Marketplace wants strict formatting. Shopify wants clean product pages. eBay (coming next) wants keyword-stuffed titles. You're writing the same record three times.

03 / Bad titles = no sales

SEO you don't have time for.

Missing pressing year. Missing country. Missing "first press" or "promo" or "reissue." Buyers searching for the right pressing never find yours.

#06 — FAQ

Things you'll
probably ask.

Still got questions?
Email info@vinylgrinder.com

Press + partnerships via the store.
How accurate is the photo recognition? +

For common releases with clear sleeve art, match confidence is typically 90%+. For rare, damaged or imported pressings, we surface the top candidates from Discogs and you confirm in a tap. It's faster than typing — even in edge cases.

What happens after I join the beta? +

After you join, we'll ask what you sell, how many records you manage and which platforms you use. Selected beta users get early access and help shape the workflow.

Which channels do you publish to? +

Discogs Marketplace and Shopify at launch, with channel-specific titles and descriptions. eBay is the priority for phase two. Reverb is being scoped with early Pro users.

Do I need a Discogs account? +

No. We use Discogs as a data source — you don't need an account to benefit from enrichment. If you do have one, you can link it to sync your collection and wantlists.

How do you use Discogs data? +

Connects with Discogs data where permitted to help you prepare structured listings faster. We use catalogue and release details — artist, title, label, catalog number, pressing — to pre-fill your listings. You add condition, price and photos.

Who owns the data? +

You do. Full CSV and JSON export on every plan, including Free. Your catalogue is your catalogue — VinylGrinder is a tool, not a hostage situation.

Do you grade condition for me? +

No. Condition grading is the dealer's call, always. We surface the Goldmine grade fields for sleeve and media, and auto-fill pressing/pressing-variant data — but you grade the record.

What about 7"s, 12"s, 10"s, box sets? +

All of the above. Box sets and multi-disc releases are handled as a single record with linked media. 78s are on the roadmap after launch.

Privacy Policy.

Who we are

This site is operated by MB "People Notion" (the "Company", "we"). For any privacy matter, contact us at info@vinylgrinder.com.

What data we collect

When you join the waitlist, we collect only what you provide in the form:

  • Your email address
  • Your answers to the optional questions: who you are (collector/dealer/etc.), main platform you use, biggest pain point

We do not use cookies, trackers, or analytics on this page. We do not collect your IP address beyond what is standard to deliver web traffic.

Why we collect it

To notify you when the VinylGrinder app opens for beta access and public launch, and to understand who is interested so we can prioritise early access (Dealer-tier users first). That's it. No marketing emails unrelated to the product launch.

Legal basis (GDPR)

We process your data on the basis of your consent, given when you submit the waitlist form. You can withdraw consent at any time by emailing us — see "Your rights" below.

Where it's stored

Form submissions are processed and stored by Tally (Tally Forms BV, Belgium), our form provider. Tally's privacy policy is at tally.so/help/privacy-policy. We may export the data to an email-sending service (such as ConvertKit or Beehiiv) when we are ready to send launch notifications — we'll update this policy when that happens.

How long we keep it

Until the public launch of VinylGrinder, plus 12 months after. After that, the waitlist database is deleted. If you ask us to delete your data sooner, we do so within 30 days.

Who we share it with

No one. We do not sell, rent, or share your email or other waitlist data with third parties. The only "sharing" is with the processors named above (Tally) that we use to run the waitlist.

Your rights

Under GDPR you have the right to access your data, correct it, delete it, restrict its processing, or export it. To exercise any of these rights, email info@vinylgrinder.com. We respond within 30 days.

You also have the right to complain to a data protection authority — for residents of Lithuania, this is the State Data Protection Inspectorate (VDAI).

Changes to this policy

If we change anything material — for example, when we add an email-sending service or analytics — we'll update the date at the top and notify everyone on the waitlist by email.
